Research Areas

Research focuses both on model systems and on studies directly related to understanding and treatment of human behavioral disorders and neural disease. Particular areas of strength and interest include cognitive neuroscience and imaging; neural plasticity, learning, and memory; neuronal signaling and development; and neurogenomics. The research environment is highly collegial, and there are numerous collaborations within and between these research areas and with other labs across the campus. There are many ways of organizing the diverse and growing body of knowledge that we call neuroscience. The Research Themes listed here are representative of current trends in neuroscience, and of the integrative research programs in University of Illinois neuroscience laboratories.

Aging

Studies of how the brain and behavior change with age are multidisciplinary in nature, and are increasingly relevant for health care in a country with a large aging population.

Bioinformatics and Neuroinformatics

One of the most rapidly growing areas in neuroscience, and in other areas of biology, is informatics, which attempts to organize and make use of the vast amounts of information being generated by neuroscientists.

Cell Signaling and Communication

The details of neuronal interactions at the synapse, including channel and receptor functions, transmitter release and degradation, vesicle formation and recycling, and signal propagation.

Cognitive Neuroscience

This area combines the learning, memory, awareness and decision-making processes in the brain with the study of the neural networks and mechanisms underlying behavior.

Computational Neuroscience

Computational neuroscience is used to model the behavior and performance of both real and abstract systems, in order to gain greater understanding of how they are designed.

Development

The development of the nervous system includes the study of both gross and fine anatomy of different regions, and the processes by which neural pathways and connections are formed.

Learning, Memory, and Plascity

How the brain both learns new information and retains older memories is a problem that is increasingly being studied at cellular and molecular levels.

Molecular Neuroscience

Cell and molecular studies of molecular mechanisms and neuronal development, function, plasticity and disease.

Neuroengineering

Studies of neural systems are leading to numerous attempts to merge biology and technology, such as the use of living neurons in electric circuits, and the development of sensory prosthetic devices.

Neuroethology

Neuroethology combines the study of animal behavior under natural conditions with studies of the underlying neural mechanisms. These studies relate nervous system functions directly to ecological challenges faced by animals.

Neurogenomics and Sociogenomics

The U of I is a leader in the new science of genomics, and applying genomics to innovative and integrative studies of brain and behavior.

Neuroimmunology

Neuroimmunology is a field combining neuroscience, the study of the nervous system, and immunology, the study of the immune system. Neuroimmunologists seek to better understand the interactions of these two complex systems during development, homeostasis, and response to injuries.

Neurological and Psychiatric Conditions

The study of neural disease in the biological context at the cellular and molecular levels, and in the clinical context at the behavioral level.

Nutritional Neuroscience

Nutritional neuroscience concerns the effects of diet and nutrients on neurochemistry, neurobiology, behavior, and cognition.

Sensory and Motor Systems

Sensory and motor systems are responsible for receiving and processing input from the environment and for producing the actions of the organism. These systems include the peripheral sense organs and muscles, central processing and control areas in the brain, and the neural pathways that connect them.
